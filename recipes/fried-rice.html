Egg Fried Rice

Home

Description

Egg fried rice is a beloved Asian staple, characterized by its comforting simplicity and savory depth. It typically features day-old cooked rice stir-fried at high heat with scrambled egg, various vegetables like peas and carrots, and often a protein such as chicken or shrimp. The magic happens in the wok, where the ingredients are tossed rapidly, allowing the rice grains to achieve a slightly crispy texture while absorbing the rich flavors from soy sauce, sesame oil, and other seasonings, culminating in a satisfying and highly customizable one-dish meal.

List Of Ingredients

  • Cooked Rice: Ideally, day-old, chilled, cooked medium-grain or long-grain white rice (like jasmine or basmati). Using cold, slightly dried-out rice prevents stickiness and allows for better separation and crisping.
  • Eggs: Large eggs, typically 2-3 per serving, lightly beaten.
  • Oil: High smoke point oil such as vegetable oil, canola oil, peanut oil, or grapeseed oil, for stir-frying.
  • Soy Sauce: Light soy sauce is standard for umami and seasoning.
  • Sesame Oil: Toasted sesame oil, added at the end for its distinctive nutty aroma and flavor.

Steps To Follow

  1. Ensure your cooked rice is cold and ideally, has been refrigerated for at least a few hours or overnight. Break up any large clumps with your hands.
  2. Beat the eggs in a small bowl with a pinch of salt.
  3. Chop all your vegetables and any optional protein so they are ready to go. Stir-frying is very fast!
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and oyster sauce (if using). This is your sauce blend.
  5. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in your wok or skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  6. Pour in the beaten eggs. As they set, gently push them around with your spatula to create soft, scrambled curds.
  7. Once cooked through but still tender, remove the eggs from the wok and set aside on a plate. You don't want to overcook them at this stage.
  8. Add another 1 tablespoon of oil to the wok, increasing the heat to high.
  10. Add the diced carrots and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until slightly softened.
  12. If using garlic or ginger, add them now and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.
  13. If using cooked chicken, shrimp, or other protein, add it to the wok with the vegetables and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes to heat through.
  14. Push the vegetables (and protein) to one side of the wok. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the empty side.
  15. Add the cold rice to the wok. Using your spatula, break up any remaining clumps and spread the rice out in an even layer to get some slight charring.
  16. Stir-fry the rice vigorously for 2-3 minutes, tossing constantly, until the grains are separated and heated through, and some are lightly toasted.
  17. Add the cooked scrambled eggs back into the wok with the rice and vegetables.
  18. If using white pepper, sprinkle it in now.
  19. Continue to stir-fry and toss everything together for another 1-2 minutes, ensuring the sauce is evenly distributed and the rice is coated and hot.
  20. Remove from heat.
  21. Transfer the egg fried rice to serving bowls.
  22. Garnish with chopped green parts of scallions and fresh cilantro, if desired. Serve immediately.
