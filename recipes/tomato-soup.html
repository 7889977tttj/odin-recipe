Tomato Soup

Home

Description

Tomato soup is a classic and comforting dish, typically made from ripe tomatoes as its primary ingredient. It can range in style from a light, clear broth to a thick, creamy bisque. Often seasoned with herbs like basil or oregano, and sometimes enriched with cream, butter, or stock, tomato soup offers a balance of tangy, sweet, and savory flavors. It's a versatile soup that can be enjoyed hot or cold, often served as an appetizer, a light lunch, or paired famously with a grilled cheese sandwich for a beloved comfort food combination.

List Of Ingredients

  • Canned crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, or whole peeled tomatoes (San Marzano are often preferred for their sweetness and low acidity).
  • Onion (yellow or white), chopped.
  • Garlic, minced.
  • Olive oil or butter, for sautéing.
  • Vegetable broth or chicken broth/stock.
  • Water (less flavorful, but can be used).
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh basil (a classic pairing with tomato).
  • Dried oregano or Italian seasoning.
  • Bay leaf.
  • A pinch of sugar.
  • Maple syrup or honey.
  • A splash of balsamic vinegar or red wine vinegar.
  • Heavy cream or half-and-half (for a creamy tomato soup).
  • Milk (lighter alternative to cream).
  • Butter (stirred in at the end for richness).
  • Coconut milk (for a dairy-free creamy option).
  • Parmesan rind (simmered in the soup for umami depth).

Steps To Follow

  1. Heat the olive oil or melt the butter in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion (and diced carrot/celery, if using). Sauté for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is softened and translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another 1 minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  4. Stir in the canned crushed tomatoes.
  5. Add the broth, salt, black pepper, sugar, and dried herbs (oregano/Italian seasoning and bay leaves, if using).
  6. Stir everything together well.
  7. Bring the soup to a gentle simmer.
  8. Once simmering, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot partially (leaving a small gap for steam to escape), and let it simmer for at least 20-30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and deepen. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. If using an immersion blender: Carefully insert the immersion blender into the pot and blend until the soup is smooth to your desired consistency. Be cautious of hot splatters.
  10. If using a standard blender: Carefully transfer the hot soup in batches to your blender. Do not fill the blender more than halfway. Secure the lid, holding it down with a kitchen towel, and start blending on low speed, gradually increasing to high. Blend until smooth. Pour the blended soup back into the pot.
  11. If making creamy tomato soup, stir in the heavy cream or half-and-half at this stage. Heat gently for a few minutes until warmed through, but do not boil after adding cream.
  12. Taste the soup and adjust seasonings as needed. You might want more salt, pepper, or a pinch more sugar if the tomatoes are too acidic. Remove bay leaves if used.
  13. Ladle the hot tomato soup into bowls.
  14. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, croutons, a swirl of cream, or a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese, as desired.
  15. Tomato soup is famously delicious served with a grilled cheese sandwich!
